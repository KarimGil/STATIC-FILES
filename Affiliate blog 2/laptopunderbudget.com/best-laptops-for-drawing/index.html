The Best Laptops for Drawing – Our Most Recommended Laptops for Artists

Mar 14, 2020 by Mike Johnson in Best of Best

Most artists like to draw on graphics tablet by hooking it up to the PC or laptop but what if you can directly draw on your laptop screen using a digital pen? Wouldn’t it be easier and more accurate? These days there are laptops available with high resolution and color-accurate touchscreen displays and pressure sensitive digital pens that provides precision which goes head to head with graphics tablets.

Now the question here is what laptops are best for artists? And, what are the essential things to check on the laptop for drawing?

Well, you can buy any laptop that has the color-accurate display and hook up the graphics tablet to start drawing from anywhere, but we are talking here about laptops that are built exclusively for artists to carry out drawing works. The ones that have all the features of graphics tablets built-in and wouldn’t require any extra peripherals to accomplish an artwork. So, in general, you need to find a laptop that has a high-resolution and color-accurate touchscreen display and the one that comes with a pen which understands the different level of pressures. Moreover, you also need the laptop to be powerful enough to run your choice of drawing software smoothly, and it should be portable and versatile to draw on it comfortably.

Believe us, finding a laptop with all the features mentioned above is not easy!

So, to help you out with this, our team of laptop professionals tested dozens of 2-in-1 hybrid laptops and convertibles that comes with pen inside the box and have hand picked few best ones for drawing in different price brackets, to make your choice easier.

Best Laptops for Drawing – Top Picks

1. Microsoft Surface Pro 7 Best Overall

Microsoft Surface Pro 7 is one of the most versatile and best-selling 2-in-1 laptops in the global market. The laptop is not just powerful from its innards but is equally well-built for varied uses. It can be paired with a detachable keyboard, which makes it comfortable to use it as a tablet as well as a laptop. For drawing, the laptop is compatible with Surface Pen (4,096 levels of pressure sensitivity), which is one of the best digital pens on any gadget to date. The Surface Pro 7 is compact and comes with excellent ergonomics for ease of usage. However, both the Surface Pen and the Type Cover costs extra which adds about another $200 to its price.

Speaking about the specifications, the Surface Pro 7 is powered by an Intel Core i5 processor paired with 8GB of RAM and 256GB of SSD storage. The device can also be bought with Core i7 processor, 16GB of RAM and up to 1TB of SSD which makes it stand out in the segment of powerful convertibles. Moreover, the device comes with a beautiful 12.3″ PixelSense display, which provides true to life color reproduction.

The comfort of usage, the beautiful display, the Surface Pen’s feasibility, and the long-lasting battery life makes Microsoft Surface Pro 7 an ideal laptop for artists who wish to use their laptop for drawing and artworks.

2. Microsoft Surface Book 2 Professional’s Choice

For the artists, who are not bounded by the budget to get the best in class experience, the Microsoft’s Surface Book 2 is one of the best options available for drawing. Considering the styles of work of an artist, or a person drawing on a laptop, the Surface Book 2 can be utilized in four versatile modes, which makes it stand out of other laptops in the market.

The device is built using premium, and lightweight materials and makes it easily adaptable in the way, its user wants to work and create. Drawing or painting is made easy and flexible in its Studio Mode using the Microsoft’s Surface Pen and Surface Dial features. The device is also capable of handling heavy graphics and can provide beautiful visuals of the end work. The new Surface Pen with 4,096 levels of pressure sensitivity comes bundled, but the surface dial needs to be purchased separately.

It comes in two sizes, 13.5-inch and 15-inch PixelSense display with 10-point multi-touch support, which makes it work like a charm for drawing and provides fantastic color gamut.

On the hardware front, you get an option to select from 8th Gen Intel Core i5 or i7 quad-core processor with up to 16GB of RAM and 1TB of SSD option. You also get a powerful Nvidia GeForce GTX 1060 GPU if you opt for the 15-inch model and GTX 1050 if you opt for a 13 inch i7 model.

Considering all the specifications, the Surface Book 2 is an ideal choice for professionals looking for a laptop for drawing or art.

7. Lenovo Yoga Book C930

Lenovo Yoga Book C930 is by far the most unique convertible in the Yoga series of laptops by Lenovo. The device is crafted with a high-quality alloy, and it comes with a signature hinge which enables it to convert into different versatile modes which adapt to every user’s usage with ease. The Yoga Book C930 is designed to be noteworthy, by all means. That said, the device comes with a Real Pen, which lets you digitize notes whatever you write on it. Be it sketches, be it notes, be it drawings, Yoga Book can take it all and digitize with perfection.

The Yoga Book C930 can be a picture perfect replacement of your general notebook, to carry, use, draw, and store. As mentioned above, the Real Pen, which is provided along with the Yoga Book plays a vital role in illustrations, drawing, and designing. The pen offers impeccable precision, and the laptop can take pressure sensitive touches, to draw with utmost precision and accuracy. The best part of the device, is you don’t have to explicitly charge the pen, as it always stays charged up using the EMR (Electro Magnetic Resonance) technology. What does it mean to the general audience? You can directly plug and play with the device without having to worry about charging or pairing the pen with the Yoga Book.

Apart from all these features mentioned above, the Lenovo Yoga book C930 also offers decently powerful hardware to run drawing applications smoothly, and it comes with a lot of extra accessories in the box, which enables the user to get the most from their Yoga Book.
